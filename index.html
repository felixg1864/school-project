Built by a young person, for young people.

"The Caerphilly Young Entrepreneur Hub was set up because of a gap that I ran into personally. As a sixth form student in Caerphilly trying to build my own business, I found that every piece of meaningful support required travelling to Cardiff, navigating services aimed at established businesses, or paying for advice I couldn't afford. The appetite to start something was there — I just couldn't find the local, accessible guidance to turn it into anything.

After speaking to other young people across the borough and finding that 73% didn't know where to get local enterprise support, it became clear this wasn't just my experience. The hub exists to change that."

What we believe

Support should be local.

"If you need to travel to Cardiff to access business support, it's not accessible. Everything we do is based in the Caerphilly borough."

Free means genuinely free.

"There are no session fees, no memberships, and no catches. Cost was identified as a major barrier to starting a business — so we've removed it entirely."

Starting from scratch is fine.

"You don't need a business degree or existing experience to come to the hub. We're designed for people with an idea and no idea where to start."

Contributing to a better Wales.

"The hub directly supports four of the seven goals of the Well-being of Future Generations (Wales) Act 2015: a Prosperous Wales, a More Equal Wales, a Wales of Cohesive Communities, and a Resilient Wales. By investing in the economic skills and ambitions of young people in Caerphilly, we're contributing to a Wales where opportunity doesn't depend on where you grew up."

What we offer.

Everything at the hub is free and designed specifically for 16-25 year olds in the Caerphilly borough.

The Workshop Programme

Session 1

Turning Your Idea Into a Concept

"What makes a viable business? We'll introduce the Business Model Canvas, help you identify your target customer, and start to shape your idea into something concrete."

Session 2

Writing a Business Plan

"A clear, one-page business plan is one of the most useful things you can have. We'll work through the key sections together and leave you with a draft you can actually use."

Session 3

Finding Funding and Grants

"Where does the money come from? We'll cover grants, loans, and self-funding options available to young entrepreneurs in Wales — including Business Wales, Start Up Loans, and the Prince's Trust."

Session 4

Marketing for Beginners

"How do you get people to find you? We'll cover branding basics, social media strategy, and low-cost ways to build an audience before you even launch."

Session 5

Legal, Financial, and Self-Employment Basics

"The admin side of running a business doesn't have to be scary. We'll walk through registering as self-employed, basic record-keeping, and what you actually need to know about tax."

Session 6

Pitching Your Idea and Next Steps

"How do you explain your business to someone who's never heard of it? We'll practice pitching confidently and finish with a clear plan for what to do next. This includes signposting to longer-term support."

Prefer one-to-one support?

"Not everyone wants to work through ideas in a group setting — and that's completely fine. You can book a one-to-one advisory session and get focused support on whatever stage you're at. Whether you've got a half-formed idea or you're further along and need help with a specific challenge, we'll work through it with you."

Can't make it in person?

"Our online resources section has guides, templates, and links to funding opportunities you can access any time. It covers all six workshop topics and is updated regularly. If you've missed a session or just want to go at your own pace, it's all there."
